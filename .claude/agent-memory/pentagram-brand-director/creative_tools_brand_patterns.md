## Archetype Patterns in Creative Tool Brands
- **Dominant archetype:** The Creator (Adobe, Figma, Canva, DaVinci Resolve) — aspirational, empowering, output-focused
- **Differentiator archetype:** The Rebel (CapCut, early Sketch) — anti-establishment, speed, democratization
- **Premium tier archetype:** The Sage + Creator hybrid (Apple, Notion, Linear) — intelligence, craft, systems thinking
- Most creative tools in the AI editing space (Descript, Runway, CapCut) cluster around Creator/Rebel. The Sage+Creator hybrid (intelligent precision) is underoccupied.

## Color Conventions by Category
- **AI creative tools 2023-2025:** Gradient-heavy purples/blues (Runway, Descript), neon greens (ElevenLabs), electric blue on dark (most "serious" tools)
- **Premium software (Apple, Linear, Vercel):** Monochromatic with single precise accent. No gradients unless purposeful.
- **Space/cosmos aesthetic done well:** Dark matter black (not pure #000000, but ~#06070D), violet-indigo primaries, silver/platinum neutrals, single aurora accent (not multiple rainbow colors — pick one phenomenon)
- **Differentiator opportunity for Zenvi:** Deep space palette with cosmic violet primary (not blue — blue is overoccupied in creative tools) + stellar white + single aurora green or plasma gold accent

## Typography Trends in Professional Software Branding (2023-2025)
- **Dominant:** Inter, Geist, SF Pro proxies — clean, readable, but commoditized
- **Premium differentiators:** Variable fonts with optical sizing (Neue Haas Grotesk, PP Neue Montreal, Söhne)
- **Space/tech aesthetic:** Editorial quality — Owners, Degular, or custom geometric sans for display; Inter/Plus Jakarta Sans for UI
- **The Zenvi opportunity:** A geometric display sans (not grotesque — geometric reads more "cosmic/precise") paired with Inter for body. Specifically: Plus Jakarta Sans (free, geometric, distinctive capital proportions) or Syne (free, distinctive geometric display personality) for display, Inter for UI.

## Voice Patterns in Creative Software
- Most tools: Features-first ("Instant AI edits", "One-click noise removal") — functional, not emotional
- Premium tier: Belief-first ("Tools for people who give a damn") — Linear, Raycast, Vercel
- Space/exploration metaphors work when grounded in product truth — Zenvi's "local processing" maps perfectly to "self-contained, sovereign, mission-ready" language

## Successful Brand Decisions in This Space
- **Vercel:** Near-black + pure white + single accent. No gradients. Works because engineering audience trusts restraint.
- **Linear:** Same formula but added light gray system and refined typography. Elevated the category.
- **Figma (rebrand 2023):** Moved from flat multi-color to a more unified palette. Lesson: don't over-index on "fun" colors for professional tools.
- **Runway:** Went heavy gradient/purple. Feels AI-generic by 2025. Avoid.
- **Failed:** Brands that used "space" aesthetically without grounding it in product metaphor feel costume-y. Zenvi's local processing = "self-contained mission" is a genuine metaphor anchor.
